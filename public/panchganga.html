Real-Time Flood Mapping & Monitoring
Panchganga Basin

A cutting-edge integration of physical Internet of Things (IoT) sensors and satellite Geoinformatics to provide early warnings and dynamic inundation mapping for the flood-prone Kolhapur region.

System Architecture Phase

Project Architecture

The Panchganga river basin experiences recurrent extreme flooding during the Indian Summer Monsoon, severely impacting Kolhapur city and agricultural plains. This project aims to transition from post-event mapping to a real-time predictive dashboard by combining ground-truth telemetry with spatial models.

1. IoT Sensor Network

Deployment of automated ultrasonic water level sensors and tipping-bucket rain gauges at critical nodes along the basin. Transmitting via LoRaWAN/GSM every 15 mins.

2. SAR Geoinformatics

Integration of Sentinel-1 Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) imagery for cloud-penetrating water body extraction, combined with high-resolution DEM modeling.

3. Dynamic Warning Engine

Automated backend routing telemetry / spatial data into a calibrated hydrodynamic model (HEC-RAS) to issue localized alerts before flood thresholds are breached.
